Lung Cancer Diagnosis of CT images

Image classification, Convolutional Neural Network, Transfer learning, Inception

This project is about the classification of chest CT scan images using deep learning methods and the deployment of an application for it. But why is this project important? Early detection of cancer provides the best chance for successful treatment. Lung cancer is the biggest cancer killer in Europe and according to the latest WHO data published in 2020, the lung cancer death rate in France is 19th in the world. It is easier to diagnose lung cancer with a CT scan than with a regular chest X-ray. A CT scan can also show the size, shape, and location of a lung tumor. Therefore, it is very important to have an application that can easily and quickly diagnose the tumor. For this purpose, the data were taken from the Kaggle website and the transfer learning method was used to train the data. Finally, this application can distinguish a healthy lung from a cancerous lung with a sensitivity of 99.4%, specificity of 96.3%, and accuracy of 80%, and identify the type of cancer.

    • Scraping Booking.com: Suggesting the best destinations in France based on weather

    BeautifulSoup, Plotly, Scrapy, Boto3, SQLalchemy

    In this project, a list of 35 cities in France is given for travel, and the goal is to suggest 5 cities according to the weather, as well as suggest hotels in those cities and display them on the map. For this purpose, the data is scraped from the destinations. Then the weather information was received at each destination and 5 cities were selected. Finally, for these 5 destinations, by scraping the Booking.com site, information on high-scoring hotels has been received. And the address of the hotels, their prices, and the air temperature in the city is displayed on the map. Finally, all the above information is stored in a data lake and the scraped data is extracted, transformed, and loaded from its data lake into its data warehouse.

    • Exploratory Data Analysis on Speed Dating data

    Plotly, Seaborn

    The purpose of this project is to investigate the factors and biases effective in choosing a person who participates in speed dating sessions. The dataset was obtained from the Speed ​​ Dating Experiment dataset on Kaggle. Data were collected from participants in experiential speed dating events from 2002-2004. During these events, contestants had a four-minute "first date" with any other contestant of the opposite sex. At the end of the four minutes, participants were asked if they would like to see their date again. They were also asked to rate their date on six characteristics: attractiveness, honesty, intelligence, fun, ambition, and shared interests.
